Links

These websites might provide useful information and resources for you. I am interested in keeping this list current, so please let me know if you have any feedback, positive or negative, about any of them, or if you have any other websites to add.

General Mental Health

American Psychology Association

        Their Psychology Help Center has a wide range of articles and guides related to various topics.
National Institute of Mental Health

        Division of the National Institute on Health, a governmental agency that has is a good resource for free information about various mental health challenges and resources to address them.
National Alliance on Mental Illness (NAMI)

        Offers information on various mental disorders as well as information on local support groups for adults and families.
Society for Adolescent Health & Medicine (SAHM)

        Provides links to articles and other information covering a broad range of adolescent health issues.

Families & Children

National Association of School Psychologists

        Under their Resources & Publications tab, they have a large number of articles and information sheets on topics in children’s mental health as well as school related topics.
American Association for Marriage & Family Therapy (AAMFT)

        Their “News” section often has interesting articles on topics such as adolescents & social media.

Relaxation/Meditation

Headspace
Calm

Educational Issues

National Association for Gifted Children (NAGC)
Children & Adults with ADD (CHADD)
Heath Resource Center at George Washington University’s National Youth Transitions Center

        Clearinghouse for resources & information on postsecondary education for people with disabilities.
Reading Rockets

        A great resource for articles and information on teaching reading and spelling for parents and teachers.
International Dyslexia Association
National Center for Learning Disabilities
Fishful Thinking

        Short, practical guides on topics like establishing a study routine, forming a study group, etc. Most of the articles target high school & college students buts some are for younger students also.

Council for Exceptional Children (CEC)

        Although oriented more for professionals in special education, check the tab “Resources” and then “For Families.”
College Board

        This organization not only has information regarding signing up for the SAT, but also on how to prepare for the SAT, PSAT, AP, and other tests. It also has useful sections on starting a college search.
